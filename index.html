PowRangrs — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 14, 2026

PowRangrs is a personal game-session scheduling app for small teams. This page explains what information the app collects, how it's used, and how you can have it removed. If anything here is unclear, contact me at YOUR_EMAIL@example.com.

What we collect

  • Google account profile — when you sign in with Google, we receive your email address, display name, profile picture URL, and Google account identifier. This is how you're identified in the app.
  • Session activity — which team you belong to, the game sessions you're invited to, and whether you accepted, declined, or marked yourself as maybe for each one.
  • Push notification token — when you enable notifications, we store a Firebase Cloud Messaging device token so the server can send you a message when a teammate schedules or responds to a session.
  • Steam account ID (optional) — if you choose to link your Steam account, we store your public SteamID64. It's only used to look up your Counter-Strike 2 match history via Leetify's public API so the app can show you post-session match stats. We never store or see your Steam password or anything from your Steam account beyond your public SteamID64.
  • CS2 match data fetched on your behalf — for sessions that happened in a 6-hour window around their scheduled time, we cache match metadata returned by Leetify (map, score, per-player kills, deaths, assists, damage, Leetify rating) so the Match Detail screen doesn't have to re-fetch on every open. This data is publicly exposed by Leetify for any Steam profile configured as public.
  • Server logs — standard HTTP request logs (timestamp, request path, response status, originating IP) are kept on our hosting provider for operational and debugging purposes.

What we don't collect

  • Advertising identifiers
  • Analytics beyond basic server logs (no Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, or similar)
  • Your contacts, location, microphone, camera, or files
  • Anything from Steam beyond your public SteamID64

How we use it

Everything we collect is used to make the app work: signing you in, showing you the sessions you care about, notifying you when a teammate takes action, and displaying CS2 stats from games you played. We do not sell any of your data, run ads against it, or share it with third parties for marketing.

Who we share it with

PowRangrs talks to three external services to deliver its features:

  • Google (Sign-In and Firebase Cloud Messaging) — to authenticate you and deliver push notifications. Governed by Google's privacy policy.
  • Leetify — to fetch CS2 match details for linked Steam accounts via their public API. Governed by Leetify's privacy policy. We only send your SteamID64 as a query parameter; no other information about you is shared.
  • DigitalOcean — our application server and managed Postgres database are hosted on DigitalOcean App Platform.

Where your data lives

Your data is stored in a managed PostgreSQL database hosted on DigitalOcean (NYC region). Traffic between the app and the server is encrypted with HTTPS. The server uses a random HMAC secret to sign the short-lived token used during Steam OpenID linking so it can't be tampered with in transit.

How long we keep it

Your account and any data tied to it is kept while the account is active. Cached CS2 match data is kept indefinitely so past sessions don't lose their stats, but it can be deleted along with your account on request. Server request logs are rotated automatically by the hosting provider.

Deleting your data

To have your account and all associated data deleted, email YOUR_EMAIL@example.com from the Google account you signed in with. Deletions are performed manually within a few days; you'll get a confirmation when it's done.

Children

PowRangrs is not directed at children under 13. If you're a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided personal information to the app, please contact me and I'll delete it.

Changes to this policy

If anything here changes materially — new data collected, new third parties involved, new retention policies — this page will be updated and the "last updated" date at the top will change.
